Bildperspektiven

Bird's Eye View

A bird's-eye view refers to an elevated, downward-looking perspective, similar to what a bird might see while flying overhead

High Angle

A camera perspective in which the camera is positioned above the subject and films or photographs downwards./p>

Eye Level

A camera angle that positions the lens at the same height as the subject's eyes, creating a neutral and relatable perspective for the viewer

Low Angle

A camera perspective in which the camera is positioned above the subject and films or photographs downwards.

Aerial View

It is a perspective taken from above of a terrain or object, typically from an airplane, helicopter, drone, or other aircraft.

Over the Shoulder

A film shot in which the camera is positioned behind a person so that their shoulder and part of their head are in the foreground, while the main person, who is in focus, is in the background

First Person View

Refers to a representation in which the camera is positioned as if it were the eyes of a character

Back View

The rear view in the picture perspective refers to the representation of a subject from behind, with the back of the subject in the foreground.

Side View

The side view perspective refers to the view of an object viewed from the side, with the camera positioned at a right angle to the object

Wide Shot

A long shot is a camera angle that captures an entire scene, including the subject's surrounding

Close Up

Close-up is a type of photography or filming that shows an object or part of it from a very close distance.

Macro Shot

extreme close-up, in which a subject is shown larger in the photo than in reality.
